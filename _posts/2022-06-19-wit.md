Chrysippus, known as one of the greatest stoics in history was known for his ability to fight, not with his body, but with his words. His wit is a great example of the mastery of stoic virtues. Thus, I state that wit is the combination of every stoic virtue, where perfect wit requires you to be perfectly virtuous.

#### Wisdom

Wit requires you be be able to think on your feet, and without prior knowledge about who you're talking to, along with the world around you, you cannot be witty. With wit, comes wisdom.

#### Courage

What good is wit if you never use it? Though stoicism isn't a philosophy to be flaunting, when it comes down to a debate or to a discussion, wit helps you keep calm, and keeps you on your toes. Courage is needed to use your wit, since you have to be confident enough to be witty.

#### Justice

Seeking virtue will eventually bring you to wit. Thinking on your feet while being calm, collected, and kind is virtuous.

#### Temperance

Controlling yourself when in a debate or discussion does not always require wit, but wit can be one of the best ways to make sure that you're being calm, and in balance.

#### Conclusion

Wit is something we should all strive to have, since it requires you to be able to use all stoic virtues in harmony.
